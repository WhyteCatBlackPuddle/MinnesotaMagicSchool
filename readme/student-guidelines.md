# Student Guidelines

Students arrive with raw or accidental magical affinity.

Affinity is what magic does around you before you are trained. Education is what teaches you what to do with it.

## Guardrails

- students are beginners with affinities, not heroes with powers
- magic is teachable but never fully tame
- Very little because they are un-disciplined. Magic by humans requires discipline, knowledge and skilled mentors.
- Students can be special without being chosen ones.
- Affinity should create problems before it creates competence.
- Every student still needs ordinary magical education: spellwork, implements, runes, wards, potions, ethics, history, and practical magic.
- The school should feel necessary. If a student already understands and controls their magic, weaken or complicate that control.
- Magical ability should create social, emotional, ethical, or practical tension.

## Tone boundaries

- The story can be human, but should not be unnecessarily macabre.
- Death is possible, including student death on-page and faculty death.
- Students can be villains.
- Bullying should not become torture or murder. It should reveal conflict rather than become spectacle.
- Curses can cause permanent harm.
- Magic can alter memory, identity, or consent, but these topics should be handled carefully.
- Possession and body-horror elements are allowed.
- Family abuse, neglect, and trauma can be part of student backstories.
- Political oppression and forced secrecy can be central themes or background texture.
- Students can be forced into dangerous training by adults.
- Romance is part of the student world, especially in later years, but sex is off the table.
- Religious themes are welcome.
- Emotional themes of the students should always be treated with tenderness rather than spectacle.
- When something frightening happens, warmth is restored by other characters caring, the hominess of the dorms, the comforting headmistress and staff, and warm things that welcome students back.

## Student writing questions

- What is their affinity?
- How did it first appear?
- What can they currently do accidentally?
- What do they misunderstand about it?
- What school subject will help them most?
- What subject will frustrate them?
- What danger does their affinity create?
- How do they fit socially among other students?
