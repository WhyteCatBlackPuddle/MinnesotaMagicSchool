# Boundary Waters Academy Series Bible Foundation

Working direction: Boundary Waters Academy is an episodic narrated audio serial, influenced by radio drama, supported by an interactive world bible app.

This document is a foundation, not a finished bible. It records the current creative direction, the assumptions we are starting from, and the questions we still need to answer together.

## Project premise

Boundary Waters Academy is a boarding school for magically-affined children in the north woods of Minnesota. Students arrive with raw or accidental magical affinity. The school teaches discipline, tools, theory, ethics, practical spellwork, and care so they can live safely and responsibly in the wider magical world.

The series follows students learning what their magic means, what it costs, and what kind of people they are becoming inside a school that is warm, rigorous, dangerous, old, and not fully understood even by its teachers.

Core canon principle:

> Affinity is what magic does around you before you are trained. Education is what teaches you what to do with it.

## Format

Boundary Waters Academy should begin as a narrated audio serial rather than a full radio drama.

The practical format is:

- mostly narrated prose
- strong character dialogue inside scenes
- one primary narrator or narrative voice
- occasional radio-drama influenced touches
- selective in-world documents, school records, announcements, letters, or incident reports
- atmospheric sound notes where helpful, but not production-heavy sound design as a requirement

The goal is to borrow the intimacy and atmosphere of radio drama without needing a full cast, complex mixing, or heavy production before the story itself is strong.

## Relationship to the app

The app is not a simulation engine. It is a living story atlas, series bible, and creative workbench.

Its purpose is to help us see the world we are building:

- students and faculty
- locations
- magic rules
- school culture
- relationships
- episode ideas
- season arcs
- mysteries
- canon events
- unresolved questions

The app should support the story. It should not force the story to behave like a game system.

## Tone

The desired emotional aftertaste is comfort, adventure, exploration, and wonder.

Boundary Waters should feel:

- warm but not safe
- whimsical but not weightless
- rigorous but not cruel
- local and specific to the north woods of Minnesota
- full of friendship, discipline, practical magic, old promises, and real danger
- capable of seriousness without becoming grim

The school should remain a place of comfort and learning where students find themselves, even when the institution is imperfect or morally complicated.

## Season one starting assumptions

These are provisional assumptions to help us begin shaping the series.

- Season one likely follows first-year students entering Boundary Waters Academy.
- The main lens is probably a small first-year ensemble, with one slightly more central viewpoint character for audience orientation.
- Episodes should be self-contained enough to feel satisfying, but they should accumulate into a larger seasonal arc.
- Each episode should reveal one meaningful piece of the world: a rule, place, tradition, danger, relationship, or magical truth.
- The school year itself can provide the structure: arrival, early classes, first mistakes, lodge life, winter, village work, forbidden places, school traditions, and the approach of a larger mystery.
- The larger season pressure may connect to the lake, the forest, the founding of the school, hidden documents, or the coming danger, but this should not be over-defined too early.

## Episode shape

A useful episode may include:

- a title
- a logline
- a featured student or relationship
- a main location
- a magical incident or school-life problem
- an emotional conflict
- a faculty presence or adult pressure
- a piece of canon revealed
- a mystery thread advanced
- an ending change
- audio atmosphere notes

Episodes should not only ask, "What happens?" They should also ask, "What does this teach us about the student, the school, or magic?"

## Early creative priorities

Before building more app infrastructure, the creative work should define:

1. The narrative voice and framing device.
2. The core first-year cast.
3. The likely emotional anchor character.
4. The season one central pressure or mystery.
5. The first 8 to 12 episode seeds.
6. The kind of ending season one is moving toward.
7. Which app features would genuinely help organize and develop the series.

## Open questions

### Format questions

- Is the narrator an invisible story narrator, an older character looking back, a school archivist, or some other in-world voice?
- Do episodes begin with school records, incident reports, letters, announcements, or another recurring radio-style device?
- How long should a typical episode feel: short serial chapter, half-hour audio episode, or longer audiobook chapter?
- Should the prose feel more literary, cozy-adventurous, middle-grade/YA, or all-ages with adult depth?

### Cast questions

- Who is the audience's first emotional doorway into Boundary Waters?
- Is season one built around one protagonist, a tight friend group, or rotating student leads?
- Which existing students are most likely to belong in the core ensemble?
- Which faculty should feel central in season one?
- Is there an older student, prefect, apprentice, or lodge leader who helps bridge the audience into school culture?

### Story questions

- What is the first episode really about emotionally?
- What is the first magical incident the audience experiences?
- What does the school want students to believe about itself?
- What is true about the school that students are not supposed to know yet?
- What should change by the end of season one?
- What should remain mysterious after season one ends?

### World questions

- What is the lake's true name, if Mirror Lake is only a placeholder?
- What are the lodges called, and what do they mean socially?
- What old promise binds the school to the lake, forest, creatures, spirits, or hidden places?
- What is the coming danger, and how much should season one reveal?
- How do ordinary school comforts return after danger?

## Guiding principle

When in doubt, choose the option that makes the world more visible, the characters more human, and the story easier to enter.
